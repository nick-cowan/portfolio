Changing thinking about accessibility

Background

Accessibility is often seen as difficult to implement or something you can do at the end, if you have time.

This was the case when I started working at the Home Office. A large department of the UK government, the Home Office is responsible for building digital services used by millions of people around the world, some of which didn’t meet basic web accessibility standards.

Dos and don'ts

To challenge preconceptions about accessibility, I worked with an interaction design colleague, Kawai Pun, to write a set of posters on designing accessible services. The posters contain simple ‘dos and don’ts’ tips on how to make digital services accessible for everyone.

Result

The posters elevated awareness of accessibility in the Home Office and beyond. They've been widely shared in the digital community and translated into 9 languages. They also featured in a book on accessible design.

You can read more about the posters on the GOV.UK accessibility blog.
