Learning about the issues behind Red Nose Day

An interactive story to help children learn about the issues behind Red Nose Day.

Background

Comic Relief runs some of the largest fundraising events in the UK. One of its aims is that every child who takes part knows why they're fundraising. My role was to create digital experiences to help young people learn about these issues.

Meeting teachers' needs

I interviewed teachers in the run up to Comic Relief’s main fundraising event, Red Nose Day. Teachers often struggled to teach about Red Nose Day because they lacked the right resources. Based on this research, I led a team to create an interactive story for teachers and children to use as part of ICT lessons.

The story followed the day-to-day life of Ngosa, a 9 year old girl from Zambia. Using video, audio and interactive elements, the story showed children how Ngosa’s life was similar and different to their own. At the end, children could take a quiz to see how much they remembered about Ngosa's life.

Testing prototypes early was crucial in helping us find the right language and level of interactivity. I also used analytics from past campaigns to understand the end-to-end user journey and create a consistent product narrative across multiple touch points.

Result

Ngosa’s story was the most popular teaching resource of Red Nose Day 2015. It won bronze for the best use of digital by a charity at the Digital Impact Awards. Interactive stories are now a staple of Comic Relief's fundraising campaigns.
