Onboarding new users

Helping users get the most out of a new system.

Background

Officers in the UK Border Force had an elaborate process for writing reports. Their workflow had evolved in response to a clunky report writing system. Rather than helping officers do their jobs, technology was dictating their processes. So when a new reporting system was launched, officers used to the old way of doing things struggled to adjust.

Using the language of users

As the content designer for the new system, I observed how users wrote reports on the old system. Seeing how the officers worked helped me design an interface language to bridge the gap between the new and old systems. This helped veteran officers to get familiar with the new system without alienating new officers.

I also created onboarding guides to help officers find and use new features that could made their lives easier. And, to make sure the system was accessible, I wrote clear labels for non-textual elements and extra assistive text, where necessary.

Result

Officers were able to write reports in half the time it took on the old system. When asked, 90% said they were very satisfied with the new system, which met accessibility standards from launch.
