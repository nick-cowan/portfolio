Onboarding new users

Helping users get the most out of a new system.

Background

Border Force officers had developed elaborate workarounds to compensate for the clunky functionality of their report writing system. Rather than helping officers do their jobs efficiently, technology was dictating their processes. When we launched an improved reporting system, officers used to the old way of doing things struggled to adjust to a new system.

Using the language of users

As the content designer for the new system, I observed how users wrote reports on the old system. Understanding their processes helped me to write an interface language that bridged the gap between the new and old systems. This helped veteran offices to get familiar with the new system without alienating new users.

To support new feature launches, I created onboarding flows and guides so officers could discover and adopt new functionality. I also helped the new system meet accessibility standards by providing clear labelling for non-textual elements and expanded assistive text, where necessary.

Result

The new system met accessibility standards from launch. It had a 90% satisfaction rating from officers, who were now able to write a report in half the time it took on the old system.
