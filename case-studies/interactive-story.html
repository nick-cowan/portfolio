Meeting teacher needs for Red Nose Day

A curriculum-linked interactive story that helps children learn about the issues behind Red Nose Day.

Problem

Comic Relief aims for every child to understand the issues behind fundraising events like Red Nose Day. But teachers often struggle to cover these issues with pupils as they lack flexible, curriculum-linked resources.

What I did

I led project team to create an interactive story for teachers to use with pupils as part of ICT lessons.

The story follows the day to day life of Ngosa, a 9 year old girl from Zambia. Using video and interactive elements, the story shows children in the UK how Ngosa’s life is similar, and different, to their own.

Testing early prototypes with users helped me identify the right level of language and interactivity for the story. I used analytics from previous fundraising campaigns to help design the user journey so the resource was easy to find from promotional materials and through organic search.

To aid learning and understanding, the story included a quiz at the end so children could see how much they remembered about Ngosa's life.

Result

Ngosa’s Story was the most popular teaching resource of Red Nose Day 2015 and it won bronze for the best use of digital by a charity at the Digital Impact Awards. Interactive stories are now a staple teaching resource of Comic Relief's fundraising campaigns.
