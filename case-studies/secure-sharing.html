Secure information sharing

A government service that helps job applicants prove they have the right to work in the UK.

Problem

Employers want confidence that a job applicant has the right to work in the UK. Job applicants want control over who can see their personal information.

What I did

Working in a multidisciplinary agile team, I designed a flow so users could view the work information associated with their visa and share it with employers. I used findings from usability sessions to test and iterate the content, and identify language that best fit with users’ mental models.

To keep the service secure, employers need to use a unique code to access a job applicant’s information. One challenge was describing this process to users - usability testing had shown that job applicants were unsure about what they needed to do with the code.

Based on this research, I simplified the user experience so job applicants would not need to handle the code. Instead, the code is automatically generated and emailed directly to the employer with instructions on how to use it.

Result

The service passed its digital service assessment first time and is now a publicly available on GOV.UK. The service is currently being scaled to a broader user group.
