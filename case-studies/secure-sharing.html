Sharing personal details securely

Helping users securely share personal information with potential employers.

Problem

Employers want confidence that a job applicant has the right to work in the UK. Job applicants want control over who could see their personal information.

What I did

I tested different ways of describing the interaction to identify the analogy that fit with a user’s mental model best. Share your work information worked best in usability testing as was consistent with other web applications.

One of the main useability issues to overcome was that to keep their personal information secure, a user needed to give a unique code to an employer, so the employer could view their information. I experimenting with different methods of describing and presenting the code but identified that the concept was not familiar to users and cause concern. Based on research findings, the interaction flow we decided to take forward meant the job applicant did not need to handle the code, as it’s sent to the employer direct who get told how to use it.

Result

Prove your right to work to an employer is now a live service on GOV.UK, with plans to be rolled out to a wider cohort of user.

The service passed its Government Digital Service assessment first time. It is now publicly available service with plans to scale the a broader user group.
