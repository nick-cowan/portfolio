Accessibility awareness in the Home Office

Increasing awareness and understanding of accessible design in the Home Office.

Problem

Development teams working on government services had varying degrees of knowledge about accessibility. Many saw accessibility as difficult to implement or as an add-on once a service was built.

What I did

I worked with an interaction design colleague, Kawai Pun, to write a set of ‘dos and don’ts’ posters on how to build accessible services. The posters have tips on small changes that can improve the experience of using a digital service with assistive technology.

I also ran workshops in the Home Office and at cross government events to help delivery teams understand accessibility and write more accessible content.

Result

The posters were successful in raising awareness of accessibility standards and have been widely shared across the government digital community and beyond. They are now available in 9 languages and featured in a book on accessible design.

Read about the posters on the GOV.UK accessibility blog.
