Access needs posters

As part of the access needs team at Home Office Digital, I drafted content for a set of dos and don'ts posters about accessible design.

Problem

Development teams working on government services had varying degrees of knowledge about accessibility. Many saw accessibility as difficult to implement or as an add-on once a service was built.

What I did

I worked with an interaction design colleague, Kawai Pun, to write a set of ‘dos and don’ts’ posters on how to build accessible services. The posters had tips on small changes that make a big difference to the experience of using a digital service with assistive technology.

I also ran workshops across the department and at government events to help increase understanding of accessibility and provide tips on writing accessible content.

Result

The posters were successful in raised awareness of accessibility standards. They have been widely shared across the government digital community and beyond. The are now available in 9 languages and are featured in a book on accessible design.

Read about the posters on the GOV.UK accessiblity blog.
